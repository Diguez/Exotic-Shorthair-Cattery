History Of The Exotic Shorthair Breed

Breeders accidentally created the Exotic Shorthair Cat, a relatively recent addition to the realm of cat breeds, when they crossed American Shorthairs with Persians in the early 1960s. Due to the Exotic Shorthair’s stunning silver color, Sterling was the original suggested name for this new species; nevertheless, Exotic was approved instead. The Exotic Shorthair Cat was given official breed status by the Cat Fanciers Association in 1967, and the shorthaired variation is currently accepted in all significant cat breed registries. The Cat Fanciers' Association does not recognize the longer-haired varieties as Persian or Exotic Shorthairs; rather, they have been officially named Exotic Longhair. These variations are simply recognized as Persians by the International Cat Association. Exotic Shorthair Kittens For Sale

Characteristics Of The Exotic Shorthair Cat Breed

The charming Exotic Shorthair Cat Breed is a calm, easygoing companion that is happy to follow you around and then settle in your lap for petting anytime you offer him the opportunity. Male Exotic Shorthairs are thought to be particularly kind and loving. Although Females may be a little more independent than males, women are just as committed and loyal.
